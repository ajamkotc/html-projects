Pasta Aglio e Oglio

Description

This classic and simple Italian dish fuses the earthy flavor of garlic with the smooth texture of olive oil. It takes less than 15 minutes to prepare and another 15 minutes to cook, allowing for a quick weeknight meal. Toss in some red pepper flakes for an extra kick of spice and some fresh parsley to balance the popping flavors, and you'll leave everyone at home dreading their insuing food coma.

Ingredients

  • 1lb Spaghetti (avoid the yellow-colored cheap stuff)
  • 4-5 cloves of fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, washed and dried
  • 1 cup olive oil
  • (Optional) 1 Tsp red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until it reaches an al dente texture.
  2. Add olive oil to a saucepan set on medium-low heat. Add minced garlic once oil has heated up and stir occassionally to evenly cook.
  3. If using red pepper flakes, add them after 3 minutes.
  4. Let garlic cook until it just begins to turn brown. Careful not to let it cook much past this point as it can burn quickly, leading to a bitter flavor.
  5. Once garlic is cooked, add in a ladle of pasta water from the spaghetti pot to halt the cooking.
  6. When spaghetti is cooked to al dente, add to pan of oil and garlic along with another ladle of pasta water. Stir continually and vigorously in order to emulsify all of the ingredients.
  7. Top with chopped fresh parsley and serve immediately.
